Internet Explorer

The internet was barren land.
The barren land was cultivated by farmers
Farmers were coders segmenting the land into farms
Farms were websites

The coder inhabited the farmhouse
The coder had their own tools and their preferred tractor
The tools were the coding languages they knew
The tractor was their code editor

A line of code, content, and data were all seeds
All the seeds were stored in the database
The farms’ shed was the database

The soil was the server
The farmer created several fields from the soil
A webpage was a field
The farmer used their tools and tractor to plant the seeds in the fields
The crop produced was the webpage’s experience

The domain was the farm’s address
Internal hyperlinks were the tracks between the fields
External hyperlinks were the roads to other farms

The farm had visitors
Visitors used their vehicles to get to the farm
Their vehicles were browsers
When the weather was good, the visitors got to the farm quickly
When the weather was bad, the visitors got stuck in the mud
The connection was the weather

Visitors walked on the farm leaving their footprints
Cursors were their footprints.

Jump over the fence and venture off into the barren landscape.


⬤

The above is a poem written by Katy Brett and Jonathan Quaade.

It exists as a bedrock for how we might explore the internet’s landscape with regard to the organic world.
